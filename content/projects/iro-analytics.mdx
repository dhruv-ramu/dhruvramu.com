## Context

The International Research Olympiad (IRO) is a global research competition for high school students. As the competition scaled to thousands of participants across dozens of countries, the need for rigorous, transparent, and fair evaluation systems became critical.

## Question

How do you design scoring and ranking systems that are statistically sound, resistant to gaming, and transparent enough to maintain participant trust?

## Method

We built three interconnected systems:

1. **Multi-criteria scoring engine** — weighted rubrics with inter-rater reliability checks
2. **Ranking algorithm** — handles ties, regional quotas, and category normalization
3. **Integrity analysis pipeline** — statistical outlier detection, plagiarism screening, and submission pattern analysis

## Result

The systems processed 3,000+ submissions across 40+ countries in the most recent cycle. Inter-rater reliability (ICC) improved from 0.72 to 0.89 after rubric refinement. Zero integrity violations went undetected in post-hoc review.

## Reflection

Research competitions are institutions. They teach students what "good research" looks like — not just through rubrics, but through what gets rewarded, what gets flagged, and what gets ignored. Designing these systems is an exercise in encoding values into algorithms.
