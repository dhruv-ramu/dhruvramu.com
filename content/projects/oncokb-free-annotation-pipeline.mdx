## Context

Clinical variant annotation often relies on proprietary knowledgebases like OncoKB, which provide curated clinical actionability tiers but impose licensing restrictions. For research and educational contexts, an open annotation pipeline that preserves interpretability is valuable.

## Question

Can we build a variant annotation workflow that provides clinically meaningful interpretation without depending on restricted databases?

## Method

The pipeline integrates:

- **VEP** for consequence prediction
- **ClinVar** for clinical significance classifications
- **COSMIC** for somatic mutation context
- **Custom rules engine** for actionability tier assignment based on published guidelines (AMP/ASCO/CAP)

Each annotation step produces human-readable output with provenance tracking.

## Result

The pipeline annotates ~1,000 variants/minute with full provenance chains. Actionability tiers correlate strongly (κ = 0.82) with OncoKB tiers for variants where both are available — with transparent documentation of where and why they diverge.

## Reflection

Interpretability is not just a UX concern — it's an epistemic one. When a clinician or researcher sees "Tier 1 — Actionable," they need to understand *why*. Black-box annotations, however accurate, erode the judgment that clinical genomics requires.
